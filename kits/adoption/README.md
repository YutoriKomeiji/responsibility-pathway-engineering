# RPE Adoption Kit

The RPE Adoption Kit is a first-use entrance for trying Responsibility Pathway Engineering on one bounded AI-assisted work case.

It is designed for readers who want to fork, copy, inspect, teach, or adapt a small responsibility-path workflow without treating this repository as certification, conformance evidence, legal review, safety review, compliance review, fairness review, production approval, or institutional endorsement.

## What this kit is

This kit is a practical scaffold.

It helps a reader:

1. choose one bounded AI-assisted work scenario
2. find the first template or template pointer
3. draft a responsibility-path record
4. identify the responsible human or institution
5. describe AI assistance without making AI the final authority
6. preserve evidence, missing context, uncertainty, and assumptions
7. identify human review, approval gates, dispute paths, repair paths, or reopening paths
8. keep non-claims visible before any external use

The kit is not the whole RPE method.

It is not a governance system, proof layer, certification process, compliance program, production-readiness process, or automatic approval mechanism.

## Who this kit is for

This kit is for:

- readers trying RPE for the first time
- maintainers evaluating whether to fork this repository
- writers preparing an internal review record for AI-assisted work
- reviewers who want to inspect where responsibility, evidence, and return paths are recorded
- educators or facilitators who need a small, bounded example path

This kit is not for:

- bypassing local review
- certifying an AI-assisted workflow
- approving production use
- proving legal, safety, compliance, or fairness correctness
- transferring final responsibility to AI
- making broad conformance claims

## First-use flow

Use the kit in this order.

### 1. Read the boundary first

Before copying a template, confirm what the kit does not claim.

A draft record is not an approved record.
A checker pass is not validation.
A filled example is not production evidence.
A fork is not endorsement by the original repository.

### 2. Pick one bounded case

Choose a case small enough to inspect.

Good first cases include:

- an internal document update
- an AI-assisted summary
- a reversible draft decision aid
- a review preparation note
- a non-production workflow observation

Avoid starting with:

- irreversible external actions
- safety-sensitive decisions
- legal, medical, financial, or disciplinary decisions
- production runtime integration
- service-specific connectors
- public claims of conformance or certification

### 3. Draft a responsibility path

Use the template or template pointer to record:

- who remains responsible
- where AI assisted
- what evidence was used
- what context is missing
- who reviews the output
- what approval gate exists, if any
- where the result returns if challenged, disputed, harmful, incomplete, or unsupported

### 4. Review the draft

A human or institution should inspect the record.

Review should check whether the record keeps these separate:

- AI assistance
- human responsibility
- evidence
- assumptions
- missing context
- review status
- approval status
- repair or reopening path

### 5. Use local checks only within their boundary

If a local checker is available, use it only as a bounded local-structure or repository-maintenance signal.

A checker may help find missing fields, missing files, or selected local-reference drift.

A checker does not prove semantic responsibility correctness, legal validity, safety, compliance, fairness, production readiness, adapter correctness, connector correctness, runtime correctness, or conformance.

### 6. Localize before forking or reuse

If you fork or copy this kit, localize it.

A fork maintainer must decide locally:

- who owns the adapted record
- which review gates apply
- what law, policy, organizational process, or safety context applies
- what examples are safe to keep
- what claims must be removed or rewritten
- what evidence and uncertainty must be added

The original repository does not endorse downstream forks or local adaptations.

## RPE function

Inside Responsibility Pathway Engineering, this kit functions as a **first-use pathway scaffold**.

Its role is to make the first responsibility-path record easier to start while preserving core RPE boundaries.

It supports:

- **responsibility localization**: making the responsible human or institution explicit
- **boundary preservation**: keeping AI assistance, human review, evidence, approval, and return paths separate
- **reviewability creation**: turning an AI-assisted work episode into something another person can inspect
- **return-path construction**: recording where disputed or unsupported results return
- **fork safety**: helping fork maintainers avoid inherited overclaims
- **checker interpretation discipline**: preventing local check results from becoming approval or proof

## Non-claims

Using this kit does not certify, validate, approve, or prove any person, organization, process, AI system, workflow, record, template, example, checker result, fork, or deployment.

The kit does not provide:

- legal review
- safety review
- compliance review
- fairness review
- production approval
- conformance evidence
- runtime correctness proof
- adapter correctness proof
- connector correctness proof
- semantic responsibility proof
- AI final-responsibility transfer

Final responsibility remains with the relevant human or institution using, reviewing, adapting, publishing, or acting on the work.
