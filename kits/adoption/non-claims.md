# RPE Adoption Kit Non-Claims

This note collects the claims that the RPE Adoption Kit does **not** make.

It is intended to be reusable by fork maintainers, reviewers, and first-time readers before they copy templates, inspect examples, run local checks, or adapt the kit to a local context.

## Core non-claim

Using, copying, completing, checking, forking, publishing, or adapting any part of the RPE Adoption Kit does not certify, validate, approve, or prove any person, organization, process, AI system, workflow, record, template, example, checker result, fork, deployment, or downstream use.

## What the kit does not provide

The kit does not provide:

- legal review
- safety review
- compliance review
- fairness review
- production approval
- conformance evidence
- institutional endorsement
- standardization certification
- runtime correctness proof
- adapter correctness proof
- connector correctness proof
- semantic responsibility proof
- AI final-responsibility transfer

## Template non-claims

A template is a starting structure only.

Copying or filling a template does not mean:

- the record is complete
- the record is valid
- the record has been reviewed
- the record has been approved
- the workflow is legal, safe, compliant, fair, or production-ready
- responsibility has been assigned correctly
- AI can become the final responsibility holder

A template helps expose fields that should be reviewed by a human or institution.

It does not replace that review.

## Example non-claims

An example is illustrative.

An example does not mean:

- the same structure is suitable for every local context
- the example is complete
- the example is validated
- the example is approved for real-world use
- the example proves RPE correctness
- the example proves legal, safety, compliance, fairness, or production readiness
- the original repository endorses similar downstream uses

Examples should be read as reviewable teaching and inspection aids.

They are not approval artifacts.

## Checker non-claims

A local checker result is bounded.

A checker pass may indicate selected local structure, selected fields, selected fixtures, or selected repository references are present in the way that checker knows how to inspect.

A checker pass does not prove:

- semantic responsibility correctness
- legal validity
- safety
- compliance
- fairness
- production readiness
- conformance
- runtime correctness
- adapter correctness
- connector correctness
- approval by a human or institution
- absence of missing context
- absence of harmful consequences

A checker failure should be treated as a local maintenance or structure signal.

A checker pass should not be treated as approval.

## Fork non-claims

A fork is a local responsibility route.

Forking, copying, or adapting this kit does not mean:

- the original repository endorses the fork
- the original repository approves downstream claims
- the fork inherits correctness from this repository
- local legal, safety, compliance, fairness, or organizational review has been completed
- local context has been captured
- local responsibility holders have been correctly assigned

A fork maintainer must localize the kit, remove unsupported claims, add local context, and preserve responsibility holders and review gates.

## Lean4 and formalization non-claims

Lean4 or any formalization track may help describe selected structural relationships.

It does not, by itself, prove:

- moral correctness
- social acceptance
- legal validity
- safety
- compliance
- fairness
- production readiness
- conformance
- semantic responsibility correctness
- real-world outcome correctness
- institutional approval

Formalization should remain assumption-scoped and structurally bounded.

## Responsibility holder

Final responsibility remains with the relevant human or institution using, reviewing, adapting, publishing, approving, or acting on the work.

AI tools may assist drafting, navigation, checking, formatting, or explanation.

AI tools do not become the final responsibility holder.

## Practical reading rule

Before using any adoption-kit file, ask:

1. What practical action does this file enable?
2. What could a reader mistakenly overclaim from it?
3. Who remains responsible?
4. What evidence, missing context, and uncertainty remain?
5. Where does the result return if challenged, disputed, harmful, incomplete, or unsupported?

If those answers are unclear, stop before external use.
