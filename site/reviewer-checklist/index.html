Checklist

1. Human decision owner

Can a reviewer identify who owns judgment?

Look for decision_owner and confirm it points to a human or institution.

2. Approval gate

Is approval authority explicit?

Look for approval_gate. AI support should not silently become approval.

3. Stop authority

Can the path be stopped?

Look for stop_authority. A reviewer should know where intervention remains possible.

4. AI boundary

Is AI final responsibility blocked?

Look for assumes_final_responsibility: false and ai_final_responsibility_allowed: false.

5. Evidence and uncertainty

Can the basis be inspected?

Look for evidence logs, source references, human instructions, AI drafts, approval state, and uncertainty.

6. Human return point

Where does dispute or incompleteness return?

Look for return_points and confirm they lead back to human or institutional review.

7. Repair owner

Who can repair or reopen?

Look for repair_owner. A pathway should remain reviewable when something is wrong or unclear.

8. Excluded claims

Are stronger claims kept out?

Confirm that safety, legal, compliance, fairness, production, and certification claims are not implied.

## RPE reviewer checklist

- [ ] Human decision owner is explicit.
- [ ] Approval gate is explicit.
- [ ] Stop authority is explicit.
- [ ] AI final responsibility is blocked.
- [ ] Evidence and source references are inspectable.
- [ ] Uncertainty and missing context are visible.
- [ ] Human return point is visible.
- [ ] Repair or reopening owner is visible.
- [ ] Stronger claims are excluded: certification, legal review, safety review, compliance review, fairness review, production approval, institutional endorsement, and AI final-responsibility transfer.

Reviewer note:

Boundary: this checklist is an inspection aid only, not certification or production approval.

Human author remains responsible for definitions and claims. AI tools may assist drafting and implementation but do not become responsible authors.
