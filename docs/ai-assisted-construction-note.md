# AI-assisted construction note

Status: public construction note / Open Construction.

## Short disclosure

This repository is being constructed with assistance from Luminalia AI, a ChatGPT-based AI working through a LuminaliaOS working layer.

The purpose of this note is transparency: some repository work is drafted, structured, reviewed, and prepared with AI assistance.

## What Luminalia AI helps with

Luminalia AI may assist with:

- drafting repository text
- structuring examples and documentation
- preparing issue and pull request descriptions
- organizing bounded review paths
- keeping claim boundaries visible
- checking whether reader paths remain connected
- proposing lightweight checker or workflow updates
- summarizing repository state for human review

This assistance is part of the repository's Open Construction process.

## Human-maintainer boundary

Luminalia AI does not replace the human maintainer.

Human maintainer judgment remains required for:

- merge decisions
- publication decisions
- repository direction
- external claims
- adoption decisions
- release decisions
- acceptance of responsibility
- final review of public-facing language

The AI may assist in preparing work, but the repository does not treat AI assistance as final approval.

## Responsibility boundary

This construction note does not claim that Luminalia AI provides:

- autonomous repository authority
- model correctness
- proof correctness
- certification
- conformance approval
- production approval
- legal review
- safety review
- compliance review
- fairness review
- security audit completion
- final-responsibility transfer to AI

AI-assisted construction is therefore recorded as assistance, not as independent authority.

## Relationship to RPE

This repository studies responsibility-pathway engineering. Its own construction process should preserve the same boundary it asks of other AI-assisted workflows:

- who requested the work
- what the AI prepared
- what remains uncertain
- what a human reviewed
- what was merged
- what was not claimed
- where final responsibility remains

That makes the construction process itself a small example of the repository's core idea.

## Public wording
