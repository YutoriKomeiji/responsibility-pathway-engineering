# Approval Gate

## Purpose

An Approval Gate is a designed point where an action, transition, or claim must be reviewed before the Responsibility Pathway continues.

Approval is not merely a button press.
Approval is meaningful only when the approver has sufficient information, authority, time, and ability to refuse or redirect.

## Minimal Definition

An Approval Gate is a Responsibility Node or node relation that controls whether a pathway transition may continue under stated conditions.

## Approval Requirements

A meaningful approval gate should expose:

- what is being approved
- who requested it
- who or what produced the recommendation
- what evidence supports it
- what uncertainty remains
- what authority is being exercised
- what effect approval will have
- what happens if approval is denied
- what repair path exists if approval was wrong

## Approval States

An approval gate may have states such as:

- pending
- approved
- rejected
- escalated
- expired
- overridden
- suspended
- repaired

## Failure Modes

Approval fails as responsibility design when:

- the approver cannot understand the action
- the approver lacks authority
- refusal is unavailable or punished
- the approval is coerced by time pressure
- the interface hides risk or uncertainty
- the approver sees output but not context
- the system treats approval as liability transfer without return or repair path

## Relationship to Human Return Point

An approval gate may become a Human Return Point if responsibility-related state returns to human understanding, authority, time, and repair capacity.

But not every approval gate is a Human Return Point.

## Boundary

An Approval Gate records or controls permission to continue.

It does not automatically prove responsibility, informed consent, legal authorization, or ethical validity.
