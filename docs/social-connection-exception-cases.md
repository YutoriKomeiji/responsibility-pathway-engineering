# Social Connection Exception Cases

This note records exception cases that may appear when Responsibility Pathway Engineering is connected to real organizations, affected parties, contracts, policies, legal systems, or cross-border governance.

It is a social-connection risk map only.

It does not introduce schema changes, checker changes, workflow changes, runtime behavior, connector behavior, legal decision logic, compliance certification, safety certification, or production authorization.

Use `docs/social-connection-review-navigation.md` for the focused reader path before applying these exception cases in review.

## Relation to approval-transfer alignment

Use this note with `docs/evidence-approval-transfer-alignment.md` when approval skip, responsibility transfer, evidence logging, or explanation paths are connected to a real social context.

The exception cases below help check whether a pathway that appears locally complete may still fail in social connection.

## Exception groups

### Explanation gap

A pathway may preserve evidence and responsibility links but still fail to explain the matter in a way that reviewers or affected parties can understand.

### Agreement gap

A pathway may rely on consent, acceptance, contract terms, or continued use even when the affected party did not meaningfully understand or accept the arrangement.

### Authority gap

A person or role may approve, accept, or inherit responsibility without having the practical authority, knowledge, or capacity to do so.

### Approval-skip gap

A human approval step may be skipped under a policy, but the skip may not preserve enough information to reconstruct the rule, owner, evidence, and return path.

### High-volume processing gap

Large-scale AI-assisted processing may make per-case human review impractical and may shift responsibility to design, policy, deployment, or organizational owners.

### Affected-party gap

The contracting party, operating party, and affected party may differ.

A pathway that explains or returns only to the contracting party may miss the person or group actually affected.

### Jurisdiction gap

The pathway may cross organizational, national, or legal boundaries.

A record that is sufficient in one context may not be sufficient in another.

### Evidence-disclosure gap

Evidence may exist but may not be directly shareable because of privacy, safety, confidentiality, trade-secret, or third-party constraints.

### Organizational-continuity gap

A responsible person, team, vendor, or organization may change through transfer, resignation, outsourcing, merger, closure, or restructuring.

The pathway should preserve a successor or institutional return route when needed.

### Effective-remedy gap

A return, appeal, or repair route may exist on paper but not be practically usable.

A pathway should distinguish formal availability from effective return or repair.

### Reopening gap

A matter may appear closed, but later evidence, harm, or affected-party objection may require reopening.

## Boundary

These exception cases do not decide legal liability or social acceptability.

They identify places where a responsibility pathway may appear complete while still failing in social connection.
