# Issue Form RPE Originality Note

This note records how the AI-assisted work issue form should express Responsibility Pathway Engineering rather than remain a generic AI-use disclosure form.

It is a design note only. It is not certification, legal review, safety review, compliance review, fairness review, production approval, conformance evidence, social acceptance proof, or AI final-responsibility transfer.

## Core distinction

A generic AI-use form records that AI was used.

A Responsibility Pathway issue form records whether the responsibility path remains returnable.

The central question is not only:

Did AI assist this work?

The central RPE question is:

If this work is wrong, unclear, harmful, incomplete, unsupported, or disputed, where can judgment, evidence, review, repair, and responsibility return?

## Originality anchors

The issue form should preserve three RPE-specific anchors.

### 1. Returnability

The form should make the return point explicit.

A record is weak if it says only who used AI.

A record is stronger if it says where the case returns when the result must be questioned, repaired, disputed, or reopened.

### 2. Non-final-AI boundary

The form should make clear what AI did not decide.

AI may assist suggestion, summarization, drafting, classification, or review support.

The form should not imply that AI became the final responsibility holder.

### 3. Repair / dispute / reopening path

The form should preserve what happens after failure or disagreement.

A responsibility pathway is incomplete if it records only approval.

It should also preserve how to repair, dispute, reopen, return, or correct the path.

## Difference from ordinary checklists

Ordinary checklists often ask:

- Was AI used?
- Was it reviewed?
- Were tests run?
- Was the risk acceptable?

The RPE form should ask:

- What did AI assist with?
- What did AI not decide?
- What evidence remains?
- What context is missing?
- Who or what can review the record?
- Where does responsibility return if the record fails?
- How can the path be repaired or reopened?

## Design rule

Do not optimize the form only for completion.

Optimize it for later return.

A completed form that cannot support return, dispute, repair, or reopening is not a strong RPE artifact.

## Practical phrase

Use this phrase when explaining the issue form:

AI-assisted work should not only disclose AI use. It should preserve a path back to human or institutional judgment, evidence, review, repair, and responsibility.

## Japanese phrase

AIを使った作業は、AI利用を開示するだけでは足りません。判断、証拠、レビュー、修復、責任が人間または組織へ戻れる経路を残す必要があります。

## Next design direction

Future issue form, guide, demo, checker, or workflow work should strengthen returnability, non-final-AI boundary, and repair / dispute / reopening paths before adding broader marketing language.
