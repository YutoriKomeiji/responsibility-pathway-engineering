# Runtime Model

## Purpose

The Runtime Model describes how a Responsibility Pathway behaves while a system, process, or interaction is active.

It does not assign blame.
It describes where responsibility can originate, move, pause, return, and be repaired.

## Runtime Pathway

A runtime responsibility pathway is an active structure through which responsibility-related states can move across entities during operation.

A pathway may involve:

- humans
- AI agents
- organizations
- institutions
- business processes
- information systems
- documents or artifacts

## Lifecycle States

A runtime pathway may pass through the following states:

1. Originating
2. Propagating
3. Suspended
4. Returning
5. Repaired
6. Closed

These states are descriptive, not legal conclusions.

## State Descriptions

### Originating

Responsibility begins to attach to an action, decision, instruction, artifact, or system state.

### Propagating

Responsibility-related meaning, authority, value, cost, or risk moves to another entity or artifact.

### Suspended

The pathway is interrupted because judgment, authority, evidence, or reversibility is insufficient.

### Returning

Responsibility becomes visible again to an entity capable of judgment, repair, or continuation.

### Repaired

A broken or unclear pathway is restored enough for explanation, correction, compensation, rollback, or learning.

### Closed

The pathway has reached a documented endpoint. Closure does not imply innocence, fault, legal finality, or moral completion.

## Runtime Boundary

The runtime model is not a compliance claim.

It is a descriptive model for making responsibility pathways observable, reviewable, and repairable during operation.
