# Value and Cost Flow

## Purpose

Value and Cost Flow describes how benefit, burden, risk, repair effort, and impact move through a Responsibility Pathway.

Responsibility Pathway Engineering treats value and cost as first-class elements because responsibility becomes unclear when value and cost separate without a return path.

## Minimal Definition

A Value Flow is the pathway through which benefit, utility, authority, advantage, efficiency, or gain moves across entities.

A Cost Flow is the pathway through which burden, risk, harm, labor, uncertainty, repair effort, or loss moves across entities.

## Core Concern

A pathway becomes responsibility-unstable when value and cost diverge without visibility.

Examples:

- one entity gains value while another receives unacknowledged cost
- automation reduces visible labor but transfers repair burden to humans
- an AI system increases efficiency while hiding uncertainty from the decision owner
- a customer or affected party bears the cost of a failure without a repair path
- an organization captures benefit while responsibility returns only to an operator

## Flow Questions

A Responsibility Pathway should ask:

- Who receives value?
- Who carries cost?
- Who can see the cost?
- Who can stop cost transfer?
- Who can repair displaced cost?
- Who can explain why value and cost were distributed this way?
- What evidence preserves the distribution?

## Relationship to the Eight Elements

Value and cost are two of the eight elements, but they interact with all others:

- meaning: what the value or cost means
- authority: who can allocate or stop it
- time: when it appears or becomes visible
- quality: whether the information about it is reliable
- trust: whether parties believe the distribution is fair or safe
- reversibility: whether the flow can be undone or compensated
- value: who benefits
- cost: who bears burden or harm

## Failure Modes

Value / cost failure may occur when:

- value is recorded but cost is hidden
- cost is transferred without authority
- cost appears after closure
- repair cost is assigned to an entity without repair authority
- value is privatized while cost is socialized
- efficiency gain erases evidence needed for accountability

## Boundary

This model does not define economic value, legal damages, moral desert, or compensation amounts.

It defines visibility and returnability of value and cost within Responsibility Pathways.
