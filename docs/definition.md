# Responsibility Pathway Definition

## Definition

A Responsibility Pathway is the structural pathway through which responsibility can originate, propagate, suspend, return, and be repaired across humans, AI systems, organizations, institutions, and business processes.

It is not merely a chain of accountability or a record of execution.

A Responsibility Pathway preserves relationships between decisions, authority, meaning, value, cost, reversibility, and repair, enabling responsibility to remain recoverable even when execution is distributed.

## Scope

The concept is implementation-independent and may exist across humans, AI systems, organizations, business processes, information systems, and institutions.

## Non-goals

A Responsibility Pathway is not a blame assignment mechanism, legal doctrine, audit log, provenance database, traceability system, workflow engine, or complete AI governance framework.

## Engineering Perspective

Responsibility Pathway Engineering studies how responsibility can be structurally preserved across distributed decision-making systems. Its objective is to preserve the possibility that responsibility, meaning, value, authority, cost, and repair can return to an identifiable human decision context.

The human author remains responsible for definitions and claims. AI systems may assist drafting and formalization but are not the responsible author of this specification.
